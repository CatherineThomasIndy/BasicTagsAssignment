Betta Fish Facts

Scientific name: Betta Splendens

Betta splendens, also called "Siamese fighting fish," is a freshwater fish native to Southeast Asia. They are known primarily for their beautiful appearance and their territorial aggression.
Unlike most fish, bettas have what is called a "labyrinth organ," which is an organ that allows bettas to go to the surface of the water and draw in oxygen straight from the air! This means that
bettas can actually tolerate low oxygen levels in the water, and is likely an adaptation due to their environment - their preferred natural habitat is a still, shallow pond with lots of plant coverage.


Care Requirements for Betta Splendens:

  • An aquarium that is a minimum of 5 to 10 gallons
  • A secure lid - Bettas can "leap" out of the water!
  • An aquarium filter rated for the size of the tank
  • An aquarium heater rated for the size of the tank
  • Substrate to cover the bottom of the tank, such as gravel or sand
  • Hiding spots such as silk plants, real plants, and aquarium hides
  • Water dechlorinator (if using tap water that contains chlorine or chloramines)
  • Food for carnivorous fish
  • Tank cleaning supplies such as an aquarium vacuum and a brush
